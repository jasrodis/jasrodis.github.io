About

I joined as the second backend hire. Seven years later, I'm one of two engineers still here from that original team. I now run six engineering teams and lead the CTO Office while the company looks for its next CTO.

The company grew to 300 people and contracted to 150. Five banking clients became twenty-seven. Eight engineers became thirty. I was around for all of it.

Before this, four years at CERN building real-time systems for the Large Hadron Collider. IEEE-published.

How I lead

Most companies treat hiring as the fix for everything. I think retention is the harder problem and the one that actually matters. If your best engineers are leaving, no pipeline solves that.

I'd rather give a team clear goals and trust them to figure out the how than review every decision myself. 100% voluntary retention over two years as an EM. Not because I got lucky with the team, but because I paid attention.
